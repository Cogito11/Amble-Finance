WHY IT MATTERS

Money you can't see is money you can't manage.

Most people don't overspend on purpose. They overspend because nothing shows them the total until the bill arrives. A clear, current picture of your accounts and your budget is one of the simplest changes you can make to your financial life, and one of the few that pays off almost immediately. Here's how Amble helps you see your money clearly.

01

See where it actually goes

Amble lets you assign every purchase to a category, so the small charges that would be easy to lose individually are now tracked exactly so you know where your money goes.

02

Catch problems while they're small

Amble's budget gauges show you how much of each category you've used before the month is over, so a card that's three-quarters full on the 12th is a warning you can act on, not a bill you find out about later.

03

Turn goals into numbers

"Save more" isn't a plan that's easy to act on. "Put $200 into savings every payday" is, and Amble makes it easy to stick to to your goals by turning them into measurable actions.

04

Make debt visible, not abstract

Tracking balances and interest rates side by side with everything else you own makes payoff progress concrete instead of a number you'd rather not think about.

You don't need a finance background to manage money well. You need to be able to see it clearly, consistently, in one place.

FEATURES

Everything you need. Nothing you don't.

Amble covers the basics of managing money well, without becoming complicated.

Accounts, all in one place

Track checking, savings, and credit cards together, with your net worth calculated automatically as you go.

Budgets you can actually see

Set a limit for any category and watch a live gauge show what's spent and what's left. No math required.

Plans that repeat themselves

Build a budget around a paycheck, split it into categories, and set it active. Have it repeat weekly, every two weeks, monthly, or on your own schedule.

Transactions in seconds

Log income, expenses, and transfers quickly, then search and filter your history whenever you need it.

A dashboard that tells you something

See net worth, spending by category, and income versus expenses over the last six months at a glance.

Your numbers stay yours

Everything is stored on your own computer. No account to create, no server to trust, nothing to subscribe to.

Easy Debt Tracking

Credit cards and loans carry their own balance and APR, kept alongside everything you own so payoff progress is easy to see instead of easy to avoid.

Make it your own

Show or hide dashboard widgets, reorder the sidebar, pick your footer summary, and set your currency. Amble adapts to how you actually want to use it.

Back up and own your data

Export a full JSON backup any time, or pull your transactions into a CSV for a spreadsheet. Your data can leave whenever you want it to. That's your call, not an app's.

One-time setup for macOS

Dear macOS User,
Thank you for downloading Amble! Before you run the program there's one thing you need to do.

Because Amble isn't signed with the Apple Developer Certificate yet, macOS marks it as "damaged and can't be opened" the first time you try to launch it. The app isn't actually damaged. One simple command clears the issue.

IF YOU DOWNLOADED THE .DMG INSTALLER

After dragging Amble into your Applications folder, run this command.

xattr -dr com.apple.quarantine /Applications/Amble.app

IF YOU DOWNLOADED THE .ZIP

Run this command right after the download finishes.

xattr -dr com.apple.quarantine ~/Downloads/Amble.app

Adjust the path if you extracted or moved the app somewhere other than Downloads. Paste the command into Terminal (Applications → Utilities → Terminal) and press Return.

We're working on getting Amble properly signed and notarized so this step goes away entirely, we want opening it to be as easy as the rest of the app.
